Macade Rebrand

Overview

Fusing Heritage with Modern Style

Elevating performance and aesthetics on and off the course. With a long line of tradition behind the sport, I envisioned a brand that meets not only the functional needs of golfers but also enhances confidence.

Role

Branding

Software

Illustrator, InDesign

Timeframe

14 Hours

Identity

The rebranding concept for Macade began with a chart highlighting its proposed values, personality, positioning, target market and voice. The rebrand positions Macade as a premium and timeless brand bringing luxury quality to sportswear with key focuses on quality and craftsmanship catered to, Millennials/Gen-z who value both style and functionality.

Brand Story & Vision

Golf may seem straightforward at first glance — get the ball into the hole — but there's much more to it. It requires mastery of course management, adapting to course conditions, and years of perfecting the golf swing. Macade’s new identity reflects this same amount of depth that goes into the game by mirroring the intricate nature of the sport emphasizing the craftsmanship that requires a lot of time to master.

Logo Development

Creating the logo started off with a series of sketches, drawing inspiration from different elements related to the game of golf. As seen in the sketches, these allowed me to play around with the designs by adding or removing elements like the flag and ball shading.

Reflection

Overall, this project was a rewarding learning experience in app design and branding providing me an opportunity to refine my skills in Adobe Illustrator. It taught me the importance of balancing creativity with functionality while addressing real-world design challenges and embedding a cohesive brand identity into every decision.

While I am proud of the work done for Épuré, there’s tons of room for improvement. Some key things I wished to have included into the development of the project are user research methods and usability testing. Understanding that UX plays a huge role into UI Design, these techniques would have helped Épuré to be developed with a user-centric approach and ensure the app meets the needs of its target audience more effectively.
