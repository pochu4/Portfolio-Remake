Anxiety, Insecurities, Intimidation - All common emotions felt by the average gym go-er, holding them back from achieving their potential.

Introduction

Virtus is a fictional fitness club designed to balance quality with approachability, prioritizing inclusivity and a non-intimidating environment to make fitness more welcoming.

OVERVIEW

Challenge & Market Gap

Most fitness facilities focus on selling memberships and increasing their personal training clients. While this approach may work for some, it often overlooks an overarching theme of intimidation when it comes to a gym environment, preventing beginners or those newer to fitness get started in their lifestyle.

Purpose & Value Proposition

The purpose of the website is to showcase the gym’s premium amenities while reinforcing it’s commitment to a supportive environment. As an anti big-box gym, Virtus stands behind authenticity and aims to create a welcoming environment that makes everyone feel supported no matter where they stand in their fitness journey.

Target Audience

Primary: Fitness Enthusiasts and Athletes (ages 18-40) willing to invest in higher quality facilities and amenities to elevate their overall gym experience.

Secondary: Fitness beginners and casual gym g-ers seeking a safe space that supports their health and wellness goals.

RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

To kickoff the project and ensure that the vision of Virtus was displayed through design, I started by conducting a quick analysis of trends in the fitness market, and it’s competitors. Some key findings included:

1. Budget Gyms are able to attract a large audience but lacks in quality.

2. Higher end gyms felt too corporate.

3. Deals being displayed at each company’s entry point makes the experience feel transactional rather than thoughtful.

PERSONA & EMPATHY MAPPING

A persona was developed to better understand their needs, motivations, and pain points. Additionally, an empathy map was created to help me capture their thoughts and feelings which helped me to tailor the website’s content to two types of users.

Branding

Naming & Logo

Virtus, inspired by the Latin word for courage, valor, and strength - aligned perfectly with what I had envisioned for this company. For the logo, I initially explored various letter-marks, aiming for a design that was clean yet recognizable. The final touch added subtly integrates a Spartan helmet into the letterform, symbolizing resilience and discipline and ties back to the name of the company.

Design

Wireframing & Rework

As per the requirements of the project, the wireframing was initially created in Adobe InDesign. These ideas were a good starting point for the final expansion of the project made in Figma which includes a component-based design system, interactivity, and prototyping.

Keyword Integration

When it came to content writing, I developed it with SEO in mind. Noting down short and long-tailed keywords relevant to gym go-ers which were later incorporated all through the content. Rather than the generic sales pitches, I tried to focus on a conversational tone that accounts for the social pressures felt by many to emphasize the safe space Virtus aims to be.

Reflection

Designing the website mock-up for Virtus has been an amazing learning experience, reinforcing the importance of content writing, UX strategies, and branding differentiation. While this project mainly focused on content writing for targeted audiences, it also emphasized the need to solve common design challenges faced by fitness brands/businesses.

As I continue to grow as a designer, I believe that this project was a good stepping stone in my career. Allowing me to experience the difficulties of tying web design, content creation, and user-centric approaches altogether. While I am proud of the project’s outcome, I see tons of opportunities to refine and expand, being able to conduct more user research methodologies like Journey Mapping, SWOT analysis, and feature prioritizations would have allowed me to enhance the overall experience and strengthen the brand’s message.
